The Problem

While developing a university certificate program in partnership with a regional university, a critical gap became clear: the program was designed for employees pursuing post-baccalaureate credentials — which meant a significant portion of the 10,000+ workforce was effectively excluded. Employees without a bachelor's degree had no pathway in, and employees who already held advanced degrees had no relevant options either.

Beyond the eligibility gap, there was a financial one. Tuition-based programs place the burden of cost on employees or their agencies. With a large, distributed workforce spanning every level of education and career stage, what was needed wasn't another credential program — it was a living, searchable hub of free, publicly available learning opportunities accessible to every single employee regardless of background, budget, or goal.

"I wanted to offer something to everyone — from an employee who may or may not have a GED to one with a doctorate. Cost couldn't be a barrier."

The Approach

Rather than building a traditional LMS or purchasing a third-party platform, TASK (Targeting Abilities, Skills & Knowledge) was designed as a custom-built competency assessment and resource matching engine — developed entirely within REDCap, a research-grade data platform already in use at the agency — repurposed as a dynamic, survey-driven learning hub.

The system works in three stages: employees complete a self-assessment across three competency dimensions; the system generates a personalized set of curated learning resources matched to their profile; and employees can save their results and return over time to track their development.

All 537 competency statements were developed from scratch, organized into a taxonomy of 19 domains and 65 categories. Each statement connects to curated learning resources — MOOCs, university open courseware, government training portals, and other free public sources — delivered through a dynamic result card system with branching logic that generates individualized recommendations for each user.

What Was Built

  • 3,351-field REDCap data dictionary spanning 8 instruments
  • 537 original competency statements across 19 domains and 65 categories
  • 750+ result cards with dynamic branching logic for personalized recommendations
  • Participant history and tracking system allowing users to save and revisit results
  • Curated library of free learning resources from publicly available sources
  • All competency frameworks, UX copy, resource curation, and system logic — designed and built by one person

The Design Challenge

REDCap is not a learning platform. Adapting it to function as one required deep knowledge of its data architecture, branching logic system, and display capabilities — and working creatively within its constraints to produce a user experience that felt intuitive rather than clinical. Every result card, every recommendation, and every navigation element was custom-built within the platform's survey framework.

The competency framework itself required significant research and synthesis — developing 537 original statements that were accurate, jargon-free, and applicable across a workforce spanning clinical, administrative, technical, and leadership roles at every career level.
