Portfolio Case Study

Certificate Program

A federally funded university partnership connecting public sector employees to fully covered certificate credentials — designed and built from scratch.

Role

Sole Designer & Program Architect

University Partner

A regional public university (HBCU)

Funding

Federal grant funding

Cost to Employees

Free — full tuition coverage

Audience

All eligible agency employees

The Problem

35% of employees at a large state public health agency indicated intent to leave within one year — greater than the 27% national average. Lack of advancement opportunities was cited as a primary reason. (2021 PH WINS Survey)

High rates of departure and lengthy position vacancies are costly — but the broader impact is a loss of critical public health experience and institutional knowledge that takes years to rebuild. The agency needed a program that would give employees a concrete reason to stay and grow, while building the workforce capabilities needed to fulfill its mission.

The solution needed to be accessible to all employees, fully funded, formally credentialed, and built within the constraints of state government procurement, compliance, and grant reporting requirements.

The Solution

A fully funded university certificate program developed in formal partnership with an accredited regional public university — recognized by the Council for Higher Education Accreditation (CHEA) and the U.S. Department of Education. Funded through a federal public health infrastructure grant with a long-term goal of sustaining the program beyond the grant period.

Employees apply, are admitted through the university's standard process, and complete accredited certificate coursework — with the agency paying tuition and fees directly to the institution. Upon completion, employees enter an 18-month obligated service period, protecting the agency's investment while incentivizing long-term retention.

University Partnership

Formal interagency agreement. All institutions must be CHEA or USDE accredited.

Full Tuition Coverage

Agency pays tuition directly to the institution. No reimbursement model — no out-of-pocket cost.

Obligated Service

18-month service commitment post-completion. Early departure may require partial reimbursement.

What Was Built

Every element of this program was designed and implemented from scratch — no existing template, no vendor, no precedent within the agency. The work spanned legal, academic, fiscal, and operational domains simultaneously.

  • Formal interagency agreement negotiated and executed with university partner
  • Obligated Service Agreement drafted and implemented for all participants
  • Full Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) developed covering eligibility, application, compliance, and evaluation
  • FERPA release process designed to enable grade verification between the university and the agency
  • Application and participant review process built and administered
  • REDCap data system built to track participant enrollment, compliance, and program evaluation
  • KPI and program evaluation framework developed for federal grant reporting
  • Tuition billing and payment coordination established with agency fiscal teams
  • Stakeholder communications designed for employees, supervisors, university partners, and agency leadership

"This program does not guarantee a promotion — but it guarantees an opportunity. Every eligible employee has access to accredited certificate coursework at no personal cost."

Program Design Highlights

The program was deliberately designed to be inclusive. Eligibility requirements were kept minimal — 12 months of continuous employment and satisfactory performance — ensuring the program was accessible to employees across all job classifications, education levels, and career stages.

The obligated service structure was designed not as a punitive measure but as a mutual commitment — the agency invests in the employee, and the employee commits to applying that investment within the organization. This structure also satisfies federal grant compliance requirements and protects public funds.
