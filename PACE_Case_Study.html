The Problem

Large public sector organizations often struggle with employee retention not because employees are unhappy, but because they can't see a clear path forward. Without a structured tool to help employees understand where they are, where they could go, and what it takes to get there, advancement feels opaque — and talented employees leave.

At a state public health agency with over 10,000 employees spanning hundreds of job classifications, there was no self-service tool to help employees explore career pathways, understand the competency requirements of roles they aspired to, or identify the specific gaps they needed to close to advance. PACE was built to change that.

"The goal was simple: give every employee a clear, personalized answer to the question — what does it take to get from where I am to where I want to be?"

The Solution

PACE (Planning Advancement and Career Exploration) is a custom-built career pathing tool developed entirely in REDCap. Employees enter their current job code, the system validates it and generates their competency profile, they select a target role to pursue, and PACE delivers a personalized gap analysis showing exactly what skills, abilities, and knowledge they need to develop to advance.

The Design Challenge

PACE required solving a deceptively complex data problem: mapping 1,668 distinct job codes to a structured competency framework, then building a system that could take any two job codes — current role and target role — and automatically compute a meaningful, personalized gap analysis in real time within REDCap's survey framework.

The job code validation logic alone required custom field design and branching logic to handle malformed entries, unrecognized codes, and mismatches between what employees typed and what they selected — all while maintaining a clean, accessible user experience for employees at every technical skill level.
